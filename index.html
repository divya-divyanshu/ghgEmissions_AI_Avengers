Understanding the Greenhouse Effect

The Greenhouse Effect is a natural process that warms the Earth's surface. When the Sun’s energy reaches the Earth, some of it is reflected back to space, and the rest is absorbed, warming the planet. The Earth then emits this heat in the form of infrared radiation. Greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and water vapor (H2O), trap some of this infrared radiation, preventing it from escaping back into space. This trapped heat keeps the Earth warm enough to sustain life.

Key components

Prediction Models

Use machine learning models to forecast future emissions based on historical and real-time data. Models like LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) or Time-Series Forecasting are suitable for this. Emission Source Identification: Develop models to identify and classify emissionsources based on data patterns. For example, using image classification on satellitedata or sensor data to detect hotspots of high emissions.

Anomaly Detection

Implement anomaly detection algorithms to identify unusual emission spikes that could indicate problems (e.g., equipment failure or illegal activities).

Data collection

Sensors & IoT Devices

Install IoT devices around the city to collect real-time data on emissions (e.g., CO2, methane, nitrous oxide) from industrial areas, transportation, and residential sources.
